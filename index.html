Healthy Lifestyle
What does it mean to have a healthy lifestyle?

  • Have PHYSICAL ACTIVITY
  • Have an ADEQUATE SLEEP
  • Have a good STRESS MANAGEMENT
  • Limit the ALCOHOL CONSUMPTION
  • Avoid SMOKING

DIET

BALANCED DIET

A balanced diet is based on low-fat diet, with a lot of fruits, vegetables and whole grains, low in saturated-fat and cholesterol and moderate in sugar, salt and total fat.

Tips for having a balanced diet:

  • Eating lots of fruits and vegetables;
  • Choosing whole-grain foods (grain bread, brown rice, oatmeal)
  • Limiting ultra-processed food;
  • Drink water (2 l/ day).

ACTIVITY

PHISICAL ACTIVITY

Physical activity should be at least 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity; or at least 75-150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic physical activity; or an equivalent combination of moderate- and vigorous-intensity activity throughout the week.
Benefits of regular physical activity:
  • Controls weight;
  • Combats health-conditions and diseases;
  • Improves mood;
  • Boosts energy
  • Promotes better sleep.

ADEQUATE SLEEP

ADEQUATE SLEEP

Tips for adequate sleep:
  • Sleep 7-8 hours/ night;
  • Avoid screens before bedtime;
  • Avoid consuming alcohol before bedtime;
  • Avoid eating late at night;
  • Cut down on caffeine.

SMOKING

AVOID SMOKING

It is well established that smoking cessation at any age has profound benefits and can reduce the risk of premature death and disability, improve overall health status and enhance quality of life.
Benefits of quitting smoking:
  1. Better physical health

    • better blood circulation to your heart and muscles, which will make physucal activity easier;

    • improved lung function, leading to reductions in any cough, wheezing or other breathing problems.

  2. Reduced risks of smoking-related diseases:

    • after 1 year, risk of heart attak halves compared to smoker's;

    • after 10 years, risk of heart attak falls to half that of a smoker;

    • after 15 years, risk of heart falls to the same as someone who has never smoked.

  3. Better mental health:

    • Stopping smoking boosts mental health and wellbeing. Evidence shows that after the withdrawal stage of quitting, people have reduced anxiety, depression and stress.
